How does practicing questions work?

Wisewire lets students practice in "tutor" mode – once answered, each question is immediately followed with the results, including the correct answer and explanations (if any). Questions can be practiced individually (example) or within question sets called assessments. See the Assigning Assessments to Students section for how to.

What is an assessment?

An assessment is a set of questions. Assessments can be assigned to groups as quizzes, homework or extra practice.

Creating Assessments

How do I create an assessment (set of questions)?

You need to be a Wisewire member to create assessments. Once you’re logged in, find a question you’d like to add to an assessment.

  1. Find the white ACTIONS button to the right of the question title.
  2. Click on the ACTIONS button and select “Add Question to Assessment.”
  3. If you’ve never created an assessment before, you’ll be asked to start a new one. Click on the blue “new draft assessment” link.

    In this example we’re adding a question titled “Math.HSA.APR.D.7_200_MCQ_WN0006”
  4. Name your new assessment and give it a description.
  5. You’ll see that you’ve added your first question. Click on the blue “Done” link.
  6. Find more questions and use the ACTIONS button to add them. Select which assessment you want to add them to.
  7. You can edit your assessment by clicking on the link within Added to assessment in Step 6 or by going to your dashboard. You can update your assessment title, reorganize your questions, and add categories.

    When you’re ready, publish your assessments using the blue PUBLISH ASSESSMENT button at the bottom of the page.

Becoming a Wisewire Author

What is a Wisewire Author?

Author is a special role assigned to contributors whose content passes our quality rubric for inclusion in our marketplace.

What are the benefits of becoming a Wisewire Author?

Once you become a Wisewire author, you may publish materials directly to the marketplace. You no longer have to wait for your content to be approved – you can choose to publish it on the site for others to use or you can keep it private for your own use. The choice is yours! Non-authors can only have unlisted questions and assessments. Unlisted content can always be accessed if you know the URL (link location) but will not show up on our search or browse pages in the marketplace. A badge highlighting your status will show up next to your name.

  • Bronze Author
  • Silver Author
  • Gold Author

Wisewire Authors will also be able to choose whether or not to charge for their contributions. Put simply, you can decide whether you want to charge for your content or if it will be free for others to use.

How do I become a Wisewire Author?

Becoming a Wisewire Author is easy! Publish 3 or more items and select “Publish to Marketplace” when you’re done. Our team will receive a notification to evaluate your submissions and will email you of your status once they’ve been reviewed. Meanwhile, your items will be featured on the marketplace under the Pending Wisewire Author headline on users’ personalized homepages.

  • Bronze Authors must have written at least three questions.
  • Silver Authors must have written at least 20 questions.
  • Gold Authors must have written at least 100 questions.

Why do some users have a crown icon next to their names?

Users with the crown icon() are publishers who work with Wisewire to help provide free questions to everyone.
